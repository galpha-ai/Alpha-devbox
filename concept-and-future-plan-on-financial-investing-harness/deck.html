AlphaDev Neo-Lab

Merging quant systematic investing and 主观 investing

One-year development plan · for users, design partners, and future LPs

Thesis

Backtest any idea. Simulate any scenario.
Automate low-value decisions. Keep humans on high-value judgment.

Quant is strong on execution. Discretionary is strong on the world. Today they barely share a workflow. We merge them into a single AI researcher, hosted and persistent.

Two camps that don't talk

Quant systematic

  • Market microstructure
  • Trading cost, spread, slippage
  • Backtest, robustness, attribution
  • Sim-to-real gap

Strong on execution realism. Weak on non-backtestable questions.

Discretionary (主观)

  • Fundamentals
  • Crowding, momentum, sentiment
  • Game-theoretic narrative trades
  • Asymmetric payoff bets

Strong on reading the world. Weak on repeatable, falsifiable research.

Three kinds of money in a stock

Fundamentals

Business gets better or worse. Most objective. Buffett's dog — not the leash.

Trend

Crowding. Momentum feeds on itself, until it doesn't. Crash comes from the inside.

Game

Sell the story, let someone else carry the bag. Recursive belief chains. Hardest money.

Our system needs to reason about all three, not just the first.

Two classes of research problem

Forecast & Backtest

  • Clear target, enough history
  • Rolling ŷₜ vs realised y
  • Trigger-based strategies, incl. options
  • Standard stats: Sharpe, DD, attribution

Scenario & Simulation

  • No clean historical analog
  • Bear / base / bull payoffs
  • Kelly-style sizing
  • Multi-agent what-if simulation
  • Calibrate vs Polymarket-style markets

What's really missing

A Financial World Model — a causal graph of events, not a spread model.

  • Current "world models" in quant are really market-impact models.
  • Serious discretionary trades are risk-limited, not market-impact-limited.
  • What earns money there is a risk premium, paid in exchange for taking a real scenario bet.
  • Year one lays the scaffolding; the full model is a multi-year program.

Product stack

Layer A — Research

Forecast & backtest any idea. Rolling models, triggers, attribution.

Layer B — Simulation

Scenario & multi-agent what-if. Asymmetric bets, Kelly sizing, prediction-market calibration.

Layer C — Operator

Shadow → small-live. Below $1k automated, above $1k human. Process validation, not just PnL.

Built on DevBox: open-source Cloud Managed Agent with a persistent runtime and a real tool layer.

Who this is for

  • Discretionary traders who want testable strategies but don't want to code.
  • Small investment teams that want compound research on a shared facts layer.
  • Quant-curious generalists with basic math but no formal methodology.

Not for: large systematic funds running orderbook foundation models on thousands of GPUs. That is a compute arms race we don't enter.

  • Per-strategy factor / tail-risk exposure

Infrastructure

Exit

  • Monthly LP report generated directly from the graph
  • Three external teams on isolated graphs

Validation — process, not just PnL

Making money doesn't prove the process is right. Losing money doesn't prove it's wrong.

  • Outcome axis: trajectory-averaged PnL.
  • Process axis: did the stock move for the reason we predicted?
  • Grading on both axes gives feedback in weeks, not years.

Competitive map

We don't compete

  • Orderbook / HFT foundation models (JS, XTX, HRT)
  • Generic coding agents
  • Retail charting copilots

We sit above

  • Traditional quant platforms (assume users code)
  • Discretionary workflow tools with no research spine
  • Scripted backtest libraries with no agent loop

Defensible surface: full stack on one persistent runtime — harness, data, simulator, operator, shared facts.

Business model (sketch)

  • Open-source DevBox core — trust surface, contributors, platform effect.
  • Hosted AlphaDevBox — paid seats and team workspaces.
  • Usage-based compute pricing for heavy sims and scenarios.
  • Team / fund tier: private facts graph, audit trail, compliance.

Users pay for research capability, not outsourced engineering.

Risks & mitigations

  • Data / research bugs → versioned data, deterministic pipelines, provenance.
  • Sim-to-real gap → Q3 measurement program, small caps before size.
  • Narrative sims that don't predict → calibrate vs real prediction markets, grade process honestly.
  • Team concentration → open-source core, AI + human share the graph.
  • Regulatory risk → tiny caps, human approval above threshold, audit trails.

Ask for LPs

  • Capital for a senior team: agent infra, quant research, financial engineering, frontend.
  • Data licensing: options, alt data, crypto coverage.
  • Introductions: discretionary funds, family offices, sophisticated individual traders as design partners.

LPs get early access, quarterly graph-generated reports, and a front-row seat to the Financial World Model program.
