Year-one plan

From MVP to neo-lab in four quarters

Four quarterly phases. Each phase has a user-facing deliverable, a research deliverable, an infrastructure deliverable, and explicit exit criteria. The year ends with a multi-user research lab running compound research on a shared facts layer, and a first cut of the Financial World Model.

Backtest any idea. Simulate any scenario.
Automate low-value decisions. Keep humans on high-value judgment.

At a glance

Research harness + data spine

DevBox MVP · ClickHouse · simulator CLI · rolling models · first "backtest any idea" demo.

Q2 · Months 4–6

Scenario & simulation

Q1 · Research harness and data spine

User-facing
  • First end-to-end "backtest any idea" demo
  • Multi-user hosted DevBox session
Research
  • Rolling regression and hypothesis-test toolbox
  • Predictive ML baselines: linear, tree, DNN
  • Event-trigger backtest primitives
Infrastructure
  • DevBox MVP on Google Cloud
  • ClickHouse historical data spine (equities first)
  • Financial historical simulator CLI with realistic fills, spreads, transaction costs
  • Versioned agent tool layer
Exit criteria
  • One external user runs a non-trivial rolling forecast by text
  • Team of three compounds research in one shared Slack on the same hosted DevBox session

Q2 · Scenario and simulation layer

User-facing
  • "Simulate any scenario" demo
  • Natural-language thesis → sized, asymmetric bet recommendation in one session
Research
  • Scenario generator with probability elicitation and Kelly sizing
  • Multi-agent simulation sandbox with configurable personalities
  • Option payoff builder: OTM calls, spreads, risk reversals
  • Prediction-market bridge (Polymarket-style)
Infrastructure
  • News / social / earnings event feed as first-class triggers
  • Sandbox runtime for multi-agent sims, reproducible from a seed
Exit criteria
  • At least one simulation replayed against a real event after the fact
  • Published process postmortem comparing the simulation to reality

Q3 · Human-in-the-loop operator

User-facing
  • Promote strategy: research → shadow → small-live ($10–$1k)
  • Above $1,000: human approval required
Research
  • Sim-to-real gap measurement on fills, spreads, market impact, adverse selection
  • Process-validation pipeline: did the stock move for the reason we predicted?
  • Methodology grading with higher statistical efficiency than raw PnL
Infrastructure
  • Broker adapters (one equities, one options-capable)
  • Sim-vs-live PnL dashboards, drift alerts
  • Per-user kill switches, capital caps, audit trails
Exit criteria
  • At least one strategy runs shadow → small-live without human code changes
  • Reason-mismatch alerts trigger human review in-thread in Slack / Telegram

Q4 · Neo-lab scale-up and Financial World Model v0

User-facing
  • 5–10 researchers (human + AI) on a shared research graph
  • New members inherit the state of the lab on join
Research
  • Financial World Model v0: event causal graph across macro, earnings, catalysts, narratives
  • Per-strategy factor / tail-risk exposure
  • Return classification: alpha / carry / risk premium / scenario bet
Infrastructure
  • Research graph storage and visualisation
  • Shared-facts layer with per-fact provenance
  • Onboarding replay for new researchers (human or AI)
Exit criteria
  • Monthly LP-facing report generated directly from the research graph
  • Three external teams on isolated research graphs

What we are not doing

Not an orderbook foundation model

The HFT-adjacent, compute-heavy prediction route is already an arms race with the incumbents. We don't enter that fight.

Not a fully autonomous PM

Humans keep high-value decisions. Automation starts at the low-value end — small trades, routine research runs, daily monitoring.

Not a generic coding agent

DevBox is specialised for historical data, statistics, and investment research. The tool layer is the point.

Not opaque recommendations

Every number traces back to the research graph. If you can't explain it from the graph, it doesn't ship to users or LPs.

Validation philosophy

Outcome axis

Trajectory-averaged PnL across a cohort of trades. Sharpe, drawdown, consistency — the usual.

Process axis

For each closed trade: did the stock move for the reason we predicted? Did the causal chain we wrote down actually fire? Did fundamentals, flow, and sentiment evolve as the thesis implied?

Grading on both axes gives us feedback in weeks instead of years. The process check is a first-class product feature, not an afterthought.

Risks and mitigations

Research-correctness risk

Silent data bugs or leakage. Mitigation: versioned data, deterministic pipelines, shared-facts layer with provenance on every number.

Sim-to-real gap

Simulator flatters live PnL. Mitigation: Q3 gap-measurement program, small-capital shadow trading before any size, per-strategy alerts on drift.

Narrative sims that don't predict

Multi-agent runs feel good but aren't accurate. Mitigation: calibrate against real prediction markets and grade process-validation rates honestly.

Regulatory risk on live trading

Tiny caps, explicit human approval above threshold, full audit trails, conservative broker choice.

Ask for LPs

  • Data licensing for broader coverage — options, alt data, crypto.
