The gap in investing

Quant systematic

Strong on execution. Weak on questions that cannot be cleanly backtested.

Discretionary (主观)

Strong on reading the world. Weak on repeatable, falsifiable research.

Two camps, barely overlapping workflows. Nobody has given the discretionary trader a quant-grade research harness, and nobody has given quant tooling a real world-model layer.

Thesis

The AI researcher that can do both — run a rigorous backtest when history supports it, and reason through a scenario when it doesn't — wins the layer above traditional quant.

Not a stock picker. Not a coding agent. A hosted, persistent research system a human trader uses in natural language, and a team can compound on.

Product — three layers on one runtime

Research

Forecast & backtest any idea. Rolling models. Trigger-based strategies. Attribution.

Simulation

Scenario & multi-agent what-if. Asymmetric payoffs. Kelly sizing. Calibrated against prediction markets.

Operator

Shadow → small-live. Below $1k: automated. Above $1k: human. Validate process, not just PnL.

All wired into DevBox — an open-source Cloud Managed Agent on Google Cloud with a real tool layer.

Who pays — and who doesn't

Paying users

  • Discretionary traders who want repeatable tests but don't want to code
  • Small investment teams that want shared compound research
  • Quant-curious generalists without formal methodology training

Not our users

  • Large systematic funds running orderbook foundation models at scale
  • Teams that want to outsource engineering, not do research
  • Black-box signal buyers who don't care about process

We sell research capability, not outsourced engineering.
